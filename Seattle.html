Discovering Seattle

Seattle, located in the Pacific Northwest, is the Emerald City known for its iconic skyline, tech industry, and cultural scene. Explore the Space Needle, wander through Pike Place Market, and embrace the creativity and innovation that define Seattle. The city is a melting pot of cultures, contributing to its unique identity.

Facts about Seattle

  • City's Population:

    As of the last estimate in 2022, Seattle's population is approximately 769,000.

  • City Incorporation Date:

    Seattle became a city on December 2, 1869.

  • Region:

    Seattle is a major city in the Pacific Northwest, surrounded by water, mountains, and evergreen forests.

  • Cityscape:

    Seattle's skyline is characterized by modern skyscrapers, including the iconic Space Needle. The city's neighborhoods offer a diverse range of architectural styles and vibrant communities.

  • Average Income Level:

    The average income level of Olympia is $71,835 compared to the rest of the state, which is $72,366.

  • Cultural Scene:

    Immerse yourself in Seattle's cultural richness, from world-class museums to live music venues. Explore institutions like the Museum of Pop Culture and the Seattle Art Museum to experience the city's artistic diversity.

Explore Seattle

  1. Seattle Overview

    Seattle: the vibrant Emerald City of Washington. Immerse yourself in its innovation, cultural diversity, and scenic beauty. Explore the Space Needle, indulge in the culinary delights of Pike Place Market, and embrace the dynamic spirit that defines Seattle.

  2. Top Attractions

    Discover Seattle's top attractions, including the iconic Space Needle, Pike Place Market, and the Museum of Flight. Dive into the city's history at the Klondike Gold Rush National Historical Park and experience the lively atmosphere at Seattle's waterfront.

  3. Local Cuisine

    Treat your taste buds to Seattle's culinary scene, from fresh seafood at Elliott's Oyster House to artisan coffee at Starbucks Reserve Roastery. Explore the diverse offerings at Pike Place Market and savor the flavors of the Pacific Northwest.
