Welcome to the official website of the State of Washington!

Whether you're a resident, a visitor, or a business owner, we are delighted to provide you with a comprehensive resource for all things related to our beautiful and diverse state. As you explore this government website, you'll find a wealth of information about state services, resources for residents and businesses, and insights into the initiatives that make Washington a great place to live, work, and visit.

Who we are

Nestled in the Pacific Northwest, Washington is a state of captivating contrasts. From the vibrant urban landscapes of cities like Olympia to the serene beauty of the Cascades and the Pacific coastline, Washington offers a unique blend of metropolitan energy and natural splendor. Our state is renowned for its innovation, leading the way in technology and fostering a culture of creativity and discovery. Washington State prides itself on its commitment to sustainability, environmental stewardship, and a high quality of life for its residents. With a rich cultural tapestry woven from diverse communities, each region contributes to the overall vibrancy and uniqueness that defines our state.

Facts about Washington State

  • State's Population:

    As of the last estimate in 2022, Washington State's population is approximately 7.8 million.

  • Year the State was Incorporated:

    Washington gained statehood on November 11, 1889.

  • Region of the State:

    Washington State is divided into various regions, including the Puget Sound region, the Eastern region, the Central region, and the Coastal region.

  • Classification of the State:

    Washington is a diverse state with a mix of urban, suburban, and rural areas. The western part of the state, particularly the Puget Sound region, is more urban, while the eastern part has more rural and suburban areas.

  • Average Income Level:

    The average income in Washington State varies across regions. Generally, urban areas like Olympia tend to have higher incomes compared to some rural areas. The state has a diverse economy, including technology, agriculture, and manufacturing, influencing income levels across different areas.

Explore Washington Cities

  1. Olympia

    Welcome to Olympia, the charming capital city of Washington. Immerse yourself in its rich history, cultural heritage, and picturesque landscapes. Explore the Capitol Building, stroll along the waterfront, and experience the unique blend of tradition and modernity that defines Olympia.

  2. Seattle

    Dive into the dynamic energy of Seattle, a city nestled in the heart of innovation and creativity. Marvel at the iconic Space Needle, explore vibrant neighborhoods like Pike Place Market, and enjoy the panoramic views of Puget Sound. Seattle welcomes you with a mix of urban sophistication and natural beauty.

  3. Yakima

    Nestled in the heart of Washington, Yakima is known for its scenic landscapes and agricultural richness. Explore the local vineyards, orchards, and cultural events that make Yakima a unique and welcoming destination.
